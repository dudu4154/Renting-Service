Rental Dispute Resolution

  1. When a landlord and a tenant have a rental dispute, the most direct and fastest way is usually to communicate and coordinate until both parties can accept the solution.
  2. When a landlord and tenant have a rental dispute, the most direct and fastest way is usually to communicate and coordinate with each other until both parties can accept a resolution. However, when they cannot reach a consensus, they must resort to the use of force. According to the provisions of Chapters 2 and 3 of Part 2 of the Civil Procedure Law of our country, if certain conditions are met, mediation or a simplified litigation procedure can be chosen.
  3. Additionally, on February 3, 1999, the Civil Procedure Act was amended to add Chapter Four of Part Two, which establishes the "Small Claims Procedure" to handle a large number of small monetary disputes that arise in daily life. This avoids the complication and formality of the regular litigation process, ensuring that citizens can protect their rights to small amounts of property without hindrance, which may infringe upon the constitutional rights and cause social injustice and a lack of trust in the judicial system.
    • The characteristics of small claims procedures are simplicity and speed, and they have made some provisions favorable to ordinary people (i.e., non-legal persons, businesses, large companies, etc.) in order to prevent the procedures from becoming obstacles to people's pursuit of their rights. For details, please refer to the appendix on small claims procedures.
    • If the dispute between the landlord and tenant involves an amount of less than NT$100,000, the small claims procedure can be applied, but if the court deems it inappropriate, it can still use the summary procedure according to its discretion. In addition, if the amount of the dispute exceeds NT$100,000 but is still within NT$500,000, the parties can agree to apply the small claims procedure and document it in writing.
    • The small claims court can be held at night, on Sundays, or other holidays. Therefore, in addition to regular business hours, courts provide small claims court services at other times. For detailed operating procedures, please contact the respective courts. However, if either party objects, the court cannot proceed outside of regular business hours.
    • There are still many details about the small claims procedure that have not been fully introduced. Please refer to the relevant laws or consult legal service organizations for more information.

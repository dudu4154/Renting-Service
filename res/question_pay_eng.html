❏ Utility (Water, Electricity, Gas) Fee Issues

Do you students know if your expenses are clear and reasonable? Instead of being confused, it's better to learn how to calculate and understand your rights and obligations first.

➥ The basic calculation concept of water, electricity, and gas fees.

Basic calculation concepts for water, electricity, and gas fees: 1 unit of water = 1 metric ton = 1000 kg = 1000 liters = 1 cubic meter = approximately 2.3 NTD. 1 unit of electricity = 1 kilowatt/hour. The calculation method can be referred to as follows: http://www.taipower.com.tw/index.aspx
The calculation method for gas please refer to http://www.taipeigas.com.tw/

➥ When signing the contract, we were not informed that we had to pay management and cleaning fees. Now the property manager is collecting the fees. Should we pay them or should they be paid by the landlord?

You should negotiate with the landlord. Otherwise, if it is not stated in the lease agreement, the landlord, as the owner of the property, has the obligation to pay according to the Apartment Building Management Regulations and the community regulations. It is recommended to clarify and include the management fee payment obligation in the lease agreement if the rented property is subject to management fees.

➥ Do I have to pay the outstanding management fee that the previous tenant failed to pay for several months, and now the property manager keeps urging me to do so?

The landlord is responsible for paying the overdue management fees left by the previous tenant. If there was an agreement between the landlord and the previous tenant that the tenant should bear the responsibility, the landlord may seek compensation from the previous tenant after paying the fees. The new tenant is not obligated to pay the unpaid management fees left by the previous tenant.

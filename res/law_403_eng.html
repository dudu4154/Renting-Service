Choosing Landlord

When landlords rent out their properties, they are most afraid of encountering bad tenants. If they encounter unscrupulous tenants, they may not receive rent payments, or worse, they may mistakenly commit a crime of invasion of the tenant's residence, leading to irreversible losses. On the other hand, when tenants rent a property, they are also afraid of encountering bad landlords or even fake landlords, leading to the loss of the deposit or rent payment they have already made. Therefore, when choosing a contracting party, both landlords and tenants should be cautious.

How to Choose Tenants as a Landlord?

When a landlord intends to rent out a property, in addition to being cautious about their own and their family's safety when showing the property to strangers, they should also pay attention to the following factors when selecting tenants:

  • Landlords should be aware of whether the tenant is of legal age. If the tenant is under 20 years old, it is best to obtain the consent of their parents, legal guardians, or custodians when signing the contract to avoid troubles arising from parental disagreement afterwards. However, if it can be established that renting the property is necessary for the tenant's daily life (such as for a student in school), the lease agreement is still valid even without the consent of their parents.
  • Landlords should pay attention to the character, special living habits, and occupations of their tenants. They should be particularly cautious about tenants who have unclear household registration, frequent relocation, unstable employment, or cannot be contacted easily. In such cases, it is best to keep a photocopy of the tenant's ID card to avoid situations where rent cannot be collected or the tenant cannot be found in the future.
  • Landlords should pay attention to the motivation of tenants when renting out their property. Those who intend to use the property for illegal purposes (such as operating a gambling arcade) should be refused, and if discovered later, the authorities should be notified to protect one's own interests. There have been cases where landlords knowingly rented out their property to tenants running illegal gambling arcades and ended up being prosecuted as accomplices to gambling crimes, resulting in significant losses.
  • It is recommended to ask the tenant to present identification when signing the contract to prevent fraudulent leasing under a false identity.
  • If the tenant is a legal entity (such as a company, association, etc.), the contract should be signed by the representative of the legal entity or by an agent authorized by the representative to sign the contract. It should be avoided to rent to a legal entity using a personal name, to prevent disputes in the future. Additionally, it should be noted that if a company's rental expenses need to be accounted for and reported for tax purposes, both parties need to agree on who is responsible for paying the rental income tax in advance to avoid future trouble.
  • If the tenant is a foreigner, attention should be paid to the legal age of majority in their home country.
  • Attention should be paid to whether the tenant is renting for their own use or for their family's use, whether it is a single person renting or several people renting jointly.

How to Choose a Landlord as a Tenant?

Tenants usually belong to the economically disadvantaged group in society, and their disputes often involve issues such as excessive rent or deposit, unreasonable rent hikes, or landlords refusing to return the deposit upon the end of the lease. Therefore, when choosing a landlord, tenants should not only pay attention to their own safety (especially female tenants), but also consider the following factors:

  • If the landlord is a minor, they must obtain the consent of their parents, legal guardian, or custodian before renting out the property.
  • When signing the contract, tenants should understand whether the landlord is the owner of the property. Tenants may ask the landlord to provide the property deed or registration copy. If the landlord is not the owner of the property, they should provide proof of authorization. At the same time, it is best to ask the landlord to show their identification to avoid being deceived.
  • Female tenants should pay special attention to the landlord's family situation and character, and whether they will be living with the landlord. At the same time, they should also pay attention to the identity, occupation, and character of other tenants.
  • Pay attention to whether the landlord is a sub-landlord. If so, tenants should understand the rental period and whether the sub-landlord and the main landlord have agreed to prohibit subletting.
  • If the landlord is a legal entity (such as a company, association, etc.), the contract should be signed by the legal representative of the legal entity, or by an agent who is authorized by law to sign on behalf of the legal entity.

Choosing a Rental Property (House/Apartment)

選擇承租之房屋，其品質、地段、設備、大小、價格、週遭環境等 ，皆因個人之需求及能力之差異而有不同的選擇。但一般而言，亦有必 須注意之處，例如：

  • 應注意房屋之結構是否安全，防火、防盜等防災及逃生設備是否齊全。
  • 應注意房屋是屬合法建物或是違建物，欲長期租用者、最好選擇合法建物。
  • 應注意房屋是否已遭法院查封，若已遭查封，應避免簽約承租，否則隨時會有被迫搬遷的可能。
  • 應注意房屋所在地的都市計劃土地使用分區係屬住宅區、商業區或工業區。
  • 應注意房屋四週的環境，一般住家或學生應避免租在四週有特種營業場所的房屋。
  • 女房客應注意房屋之防盜設備是否安全。
  • 應注意承租之房屋的樓層是否是頂樓加蓋，若為地下室是否是防空避難室。
  • 應了解房屋內所提供之既有設備，如衛浴、熱水器、桌椅、洗衣機、脫水機、冰箱、廚房、冷氣等是否可供房客使用，及電錶是共用、或各自計算等。

為了慎重起見，房客可以記下門牌號碼，直接至當地地政事務所申 請該建物的登記簿謄本。從登記簿謄本可以看出誰是所有權人，有無遭 法院查封…等各種情況。不動產登記簿是公開的，任何第三人都可以調 閱，請求地政事務所交付謄本，所以房客無須顧忌，或擔心遭拒絕。但 是有的建築物是沒有登記的，如果是三、四十年以上的房子，有可能是 屬於未登記建物，則不算違章建築。如果是新屋、頂樓加蓋（沒有門牌 ）、或年代沒如此久，那極可能是違章建築，隨時會有遭拆除的危險。

❏ Security Deposit, Rental Deposit, Rental Payment Issue

The issues of security deposit, advance payment, and rent payment are the most common problems among students, often resulting in situations where payment is refused. Please read carefully to avoid repeating the same mistakes.

➥ What are the differences between security deposit, rental deposit, and rental payment?

Security Deposit:
It is a commitment made before the fulfillment of the contract, usually when the tenant intends to rent the property after viewing it, and the landlord is willing to rent it to them. The tenant will pay a certain amount of money to the landlord in advance to ensure that both parties will fulfill the lease. Article 248 of the Civil Code stipulates that 'if one of the parties to a contract receives a deposit from a third party, it is presumed that the contract has been established.'

Rental Deposit:
The official legal term for it is 'rental deposit', but it is commonly referred to as a security deposit, guarantee deposit, or collateral deposit, etc. Its purpose is to guarantee the tenant's payment of rent, compensation for any damages caused to the property by the tenant, and the return of the property by the tenant at the end of the lease term.

Rental Payment:
The amount of money that must be paid regularly to the landlord for renting a house, until the end of the lease period. It is usually paid on a regular basis, such as monthly, quarterly, or by semester.

➥ How much is reasonable for the landlord to collect as rental deposit or security deposit?

Seccurity Deposit:
There is no set limit on the amount of the deposit, and landlords usually set the amount and require tenants to pay it. Some landlords may require a deposit of tens of thousands of dollars, while others may only ask for 500 or 1,000 dollars. In fact, regardless of the amount of the deposit, it represents a commitment to the lease, and the amount should be determined based on the strength of the leasing intentions of both parties. Otherwise, if one party backs out, according to Article 249 of the Civil Code, the fault lies with that party and the other party can request confiscation or double return of the deposit.

Rental Deposit:
Although it is common practice in the private sector to require a deposit of three months or even more, according to Article 99 of the Land Law, the deposit may not exceed twice the rent (equivalent to two months' rent). The excess portion can be claimed by the tenant as payment for rent. However, in practice, when tenants exercise their right to offset rent, they should first notify and communicate with the landlord to avoid disputes.

➥ What should be noted when paying a security deposit?

Unless otherwise agreed, the deposit paid by the tenant should be refunded or can be used as part of the rent or deposit when fulfilling the contract. Therefore, when paying the deposit, please remember to ask the landlord to sign the receipt (sample deposit receipt), indicating the amount of the deposit paid, the names, ID numbers, and deposit retention period of both the payee and the payer, and the compensation method for violating the agreement, in order to avoid situations where one party refuses to rent after paying the deposit but cannot claim compensation.

➥ After paying the deposit, can the deposit be refunded or compensated if one party backs out of the rental agreement?

According to Article 249 of the Civil Code, "When a contract cannot be performed due to reasons attributable to the party who paid the earnest money, the earnest money shall not be claimed for refund..." In other words, when the tenant (the party who paid the earnest money) fails to fulfill the promise to rent the house, the landlord cannot be requested to return the earnest money.

'When a contract cannot be performed due to the fault of the party who received the deposit, the other party shall be entitled to a refund of twice the amount of the deposit received; ...' That is to say, when a landlord (the party who received the deposit) cannot fulfill the promise to rent the house to the tenant, the tenant can demand double compensation from the landlord.

'If a contract cannot be performed due to a cause not attributable to either party, the deposit shall be returned'. This means that if unexpected events such as the 921 earthquake, flooding caused by typhoons, or unforeseen details that were not discussed during the house viewing lead to either party being unwilling to rent the house, the other party cannot withhold the deposit or demand double compensation.

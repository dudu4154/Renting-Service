Rental-related Legal Provisions

Title of the regulation: Civil Code, Section 3, Effectiveness of Obligations.

  • Article 248: If one party to a contract has received earnest money from the other party, it shall be presumed that the contract has been concluded.
  • Article 249: Unless otherwise agreed by the parties, the following provisions shall apply to earnest money:
    1. When the contract is fulfilled, the earnest money shall be returned or used as a part of the payment.
    2. If a party who has paid an earnest deposit is responsible for the failure to fulfill the contract, the earnest deposit cannot be claimed for return.
    3. If a contract cannot be performed due to the fault of the party who received the deposit, that party shall return the deposit received doubled.
    4. In case the contract cannot be performed due to reasons not attributable to either party, the earnest money shall be returned.

Title of the Regulation: Civil Code, Section 5, Lease

  • Article 421: Definition of Lease

    A lease is a contract in which one party agrees to rent out a property to another party for use and enjoyment, while the other party agrees to pay rent. Rent may be paid in the form of money or the proceeds from the leased property.

  • Article 422: Form of Real Estate Lease Contract

    Lease agreements for real property with a term exceeding one year shall be made in writing. If the agreement is not made in writing, it shall be deemed to be a lease without a definite term.

  • Article 422-1:

    The lessee of a leased land or building may request the lessor to register the land-use rights after the contract is concluded.

  • Article 423: Delivery and Maintenance Obligation of Leased Property

    The lessor shall deliver to the lessee the leased property that conforms to the agreed upon use and benefit, and shall maintain its condition suitable for the agreed upon use and benefit during the term of the lease.

  • Article 424: Termination Right of the Lessee

    After the lessor delivers the leased property, even if they transfer their ownership to a third party, the lease contract shall continue to exist for the transferee.

    The preceding provision does not apply to non-notarized real estate lease contracts with a term of more than five years or without a fixed term.

  • Article 425-1:

    When a person owns both the land and the building on the land, and only transfers the ownership of the land or the building to another person, or transfers the ownership of the land and building separately or at different times to different persons, there shall be deemed to be a lease relationship between the transferee of the land or the building and the transferor during the period of use of the building. The period of such lease shall not be subject to the limitation provided in the first paragraph of Article 449. When the parties cannot agree on the amount of rent in the preceding paragraph, they may apply to the court for a determination.

  • Article 426:

    If the lessor creates a property right over the leased property that interferes with the lessee's use and enjoyment, the provisions of Article 425 shall apply mutatis mutandis.

  • Article 426-1:

    When a lessee transfers the ownership of a building constructed on a leased land, the lease contract for the land shall continue to exist between the lessor and the new owner of the building.

  • Article 426-2:

    When a tenant leases land to build a house on it, the tenant's lease agreement shall remain in force for the buyer of the house upon transfer of ownership. When the lessor sells the land, the tenant has a preemptive right to purchase under the same conditions. When the tenant sells the house, the landowner has a preemptive right to purchase under the same conditions.

    In the preceding paragraph, the seller shall give written notice of the sale conditions to the priority buyer. If the priority buyer fails to submit a written confirmation of purchase within ten days after receiving the notice, it shall be deemed as a waiver.

    The seller who transfers ownership without notifying the preemptive buyer in writing cannot override the preemptive buyer's right.

  • Article 427: Burden of Rental Property Tax Donation

    All taxes and donations applicable to rental properties shall be borne by the lessor.

  • Article 429: Lessor's Obligation to Repair

    The lessor shall be responsible for the repair of the rental property, unless otherwise agreed upon in the contract or customary practice. The lessee shall not refuse necessary actions taken by the lessor to preserve the rental property.

  • Article 430: Effect of Non-performance of Obligation to Repair

    During the existence of the lease relationship, if repairs to the rental property are necessary and are the responsibility of the lessor, the lessee may give the lessor a reasonable deadline to perform the repairs. If the lessor fails to make the repairs within the deadline, the lessee may terminate the contract, carry out the repairs themselves and request the lessor to reimburse the expenses, or deduct the expenses from the rent.

  • Article 431: Reimbursement of Beneficial Expenses and Retrieval of Work Objects

    If the lessee has incurred beneficial expenses in relation to the rental property, resulting in an increase in the value of the property, and the lessor is aware of such circumstances but does not express objection, the lessor shall reimburse the expenses upon termination of the lease relationship, but only up to the amount of the increased value of the property. The lessee may retrieve any work objects they have added to the rental property, but must restore the rental property to its original condition.

  • Article 432: Lessee's Duty to Safeguard

    The lessee shall safeguard the rental property with the care of a good manager. If the rental property is productive, the lessee shall maintain its productivity.

    If the lessee violates the obligations set out in the preceding paragraph and causes damage or loss to the rental property, they shall be liable for compensation. However, this does not apply if the change or damage is due to the agreed-upon method of use or the nature of the property.

  • Article 433: Responsibility for Third-Party Acts

    If the cohabitant of the lessee or a third party allowed by the lessee to use or derive benefits from the rental property causes damage or loss to the rental property due to their fault, the lessee shall be liable for compensation.

  • Article 434: Liability for Fire Damage

    If the leased property is destroyed or lost due to the tenant's gross negligence causing a fire, the tenant shall be liable for damages and compensation to the lessor.

  • Article 435: Effect of Partial Destruction of the Leased Property

    During the term of the lease, if a part of the leased property is destroyed due to reasons not attributable to the lessee, the lessee may request a reduction of the rent in proportion to the part that is destroyed. If the remaining part of the leased property is unable to serve the purpose of the lease, the lessee may terminate the contract.

  • Article 436: Effects of Defects in Rights

    The preceding article shall apply mutatis mutandis when the lessee is unable to use or enjoy the lease as agreed upon due to a third party claiming a right to the leased property.

  • Article 437: Notification Obligations of Lessee

    During the continuance of the lease, if repairs to the leased property are necessary and should be borne by the lessor, or if it is necessary to install equipment to prevent harm, or if a third party asserts a right over the leased property, the lessee shall immediately notify the lessor, unless the lessor is already aware of it.

    If the lessee fails to give the notification specified in the preceding article, which results in the lessor being unable to remedy the situation in a timely manner, the lessee shall compensate the lessor for the damages incurred as a result.

  • Article 438: Methods and Consequences of the Lessee's Use and Enjoyment of the Leased Property

    The lessee shall use and enjoy the leased property in accordance with the agreed method. If there is no agreed method, the lessee shall use and enjoy the leased property according to the nature of the leased property.

    The lessee shall use and enjoy the leased property in accordance with the agreed methods. In the absence of agreed methods, the methods shall be determined according to the nature of the leased property. If the lessee violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph and continues to use and enjoy the leased property despite the landlord's intervention, the landlord may terminate the contract.

  • Article 439: Payment Period of Rent

    The lessee shall pay the rent on the agreed date. In the absence of an agreement, the rent shall be paid according to custom. If there is no agreement or custom, the rent shall be paid upon the expiration of the lease term.

    If the rent is to be paid in installments, each installment shall be paid upon its expiration. If the rental income of the leased property is seasonal, the rent shall be paid at the end of the season.

  • Article 440: Effect of Delayed Rent Payment

    If the lessee is in delay in paying rent, the lessor may set a reasonable period of time to urge the lessee to pay rent. If the lessee does not make payment within the prescribed period, the lessor may terminate the contract.

    For leased property that is a house, the contract shall not be terminated in accordance with the preceding paragraph unless the total amount of the rent overdue is equivalent to two rental periods. If the rent is payable at the beginning of each rental period, the contract may be terminated only after the lessee has been delayed for more than two months. For the lease of a land for building, the provisions of the preceding paragraph shall apply when the total amount of overdue rent is equal to two years' rent.

  • Article 441: Renewal of Rent Payment

    The lessee shall not be exempted from the obligation to pay rent due to his/her own reasons that result in the inability to use or enjoy all or part of the leased property.

  • Article 442: Request for Increase or Decrease of Rent in Real Estate Leases

    If the leased property is real estate, the parties may apply to the court to adjust the rent due to changes in its value. However, this provision does not apply if the lease has a fixed term.

  • Article 443: Effectiveness of Subleasing (1)

    The lessee shall not sublease the leased property to another person without the consent of the lessor. However, in the case of leased property that is a house, unless otherwise agreed upon, the lessee may sublease a part of it to another person.

    If the lessee violates the provisions of the preceding paragraph and subleases the leased property to another person without the lessor's consent, the lessor may terminate the contract.

  • Article 444: Effectiveness of Subleasing (2)

    Where the lessee sublets the leased property to another person in accordance with the preceding article, the lease relationship between the lessee and the lessor shall continue.

    The lessee shall be liable for damages arising from reasons for which the sublessee is responsible and shall bear the compensation responsibility.

  • Article 449: Maximum Duration of Lease

    The term of a lease agreement shall not exceed twenty years. If it exceeds twenty years, it shall be shortened to twenty years.

    The parties may renew the term of the lease agreement.

    The provisions of paragraph 1 do not apply to leases of land for building houses.

  • Article 450: Termination of Lease Contract

    The lease agreement with a fixed term shall expire upon the expiration of such term.

    If the lease agreement is without a fixed term, either party may terminate it at any time, subject to the customs beneficial to the lessee.

    The termination of the preceding paragraph shall be notified in advance according to the customs. However, if the rent of the immovable property is to be paid on a weekly, half-monthly, or monthly basis, the lessor shall specify the last day of the week, half-month, or month in the calendar as the termination date of the contract and shall give the lessee notice at least one week, half-month, or month in advance

  • Article 451: Implied Renewal of Lease Agreement

    If the lessee continues to use and enjoy the leased property after the expiration of the lease term, and the lessor does not express any objection, it shall be deemed that the lease agreement is renewed for an indefinite term.

  • Article 453: Termination of Fixed-term Lease Contract

    In a lease agreement with a fixed term, if the parties have agreed that either party may terminate the agreement before the expiration of the term, the termination of the agreement shall be notified in advance in accordance with the provisions of Article 450, paragraph 3.

  • Article 455: Return of the Leased Property

    After the termination of the lease relationship, the lessee shall return the leased property. If the leased property is productive, the lessee shall also maintain its productive state and return it to the lessor.

  • Article 457-1:

    The lessor of cultivated land shall not collect rent in advance.

    If the lessee cannot pay the full amount of the rent due on time, but pays a portion of it, the lessor shall not refuse to accept it.

  • Article 458:

    Termination of lease of cultivated land before the expiration of the lease period by the lessor may occur in one of the following circumstances:

    1. If the tenant dies without any successor or if the successor is unable to cultivate the land.
    2. Situation when the lessee is unable to continue cultivation for more than one year, except for reasons of force majeure.
    3. Where the lessee sublets all or part of the cultivated land to another person.
    4. Total amount of rent arrears reaching two years.
    5. The land used for agriculture is converted or altered to non-agricultural use in accordance with the law.
  • Article 459:

    In the case of a non-fixed term lease for agricultural land, the lessor may terminate the contract only if: the circumstances specified in the preceding articles occur, the lessee violates the provisions of Article 432 or the second paragraph of Article 462.

  • Article 460:

    When the lessor of farmland sells or pledges the farmland, the lessee has the right to purchase or pledge under the same conditions.

    The provisions of Paragraphs 2 and 3 of Article 426-2 shall apply mutatis mutandis to the preceding paragraph.

  • Article 461-1:

    The lessee of agricultural land may make improvements to increase the productivity or convenience of farming, while maintaining the original nature and effectiveness of the land.

    However, the lessee shall notify the lessor in writing of the items and amount of expenses for the improvements.

    When the lessee returns the land, the lessee may request the lessor to return the expenses incurred for the improvements, but only up to the price of the part that has not lost its effectiveness.

  • Article 463-1:

    This section applies mutatis mutandis to the lease of rights.

Civil Procedure Law (Small Claims Procedure)

  • Article 436-8:

    In lawsuits concerning claims for payment of money or other forms of valuable securities or assets, where the amount or value of the subject matter is less than NTD 100,000, the small claims procedure as provided in this chapter shall apply.

    If the court finds that the small claims procedure is not appropriate, it may, according to its discretion, issue an order to adopt the summary procedure and continue the trial before the original judge.

    The order mentioned in the preceding paragraph cannot be appealed.

    For lawsuits under the first paragraph, if the amount or value of the subject matter is less than NTD 500,000, the parties may agree to use the small claims procedure, and such agreement shall be in writing.

  • Article 436-9:

    If one of the parties in a small claims case is a legal entity or a merchant, and the place of debt performance or the agreed court of first instance jurisdiction for the same type of contract is stipulated in the terms of the contract, the provisions of Article 12 or Article 24 shall not apply. However, if both parties are legal entities or merchants, this restriction does not apply.

  • Article 436-10:

    The plaintiff who initiates the small claims procedure may use a standardized complaint form, the format of which shall be determined by the Judicial Yuan.

  • Article 436-11:

    The small claims procedure may be conducted during nighttime, weekends, or other holidays. However, if any party objects, this restriction does not apply.

  • Article 436-12:

    According to Article 436-8, in cases where mediation procedures are required by law, if one party fails to attend the mediation session without justifiable reasons after being legally notified at least five days before the session, the court may, upon the request of the attending party, proceed to trial on the date of the mediation session, and may render a judgment based on the arguments of the attending party.

    The notice of mediation session shall also indicate the consequence of the failure to attend as stated in the preceding paragraph.

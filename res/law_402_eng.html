Notarization of House Lease Contract

A written contract should be made for the rental of a property, and after notarization, the landlord can request the court to enforce the return of the property, payment of rent and penalty fees, and the return of the security deposit from the tenant without the need for litigation. This ensures protection of the rights of both parties and helps avoid disputes. The application procedure is as follows:

  1. Please purchase a notarization request form at the service counter of the local district court notary office in your jurisdiction. The cost is two New Taiwan Dollars per form.
  2. Instructions for completing the request form:
    • Please provide the names, nationalities, unified identification numbers, and addresses of both parties (the landlord and the tenant) in the request form. If the tenant has a guarantor, the guarantor's information should be provided after the tenant's information.
    • In the column for 'legal acts or private factual rights to be notarized', please only write 'Requesting notarization of the lease contract for the rental of the property between the parties'.
    • In the 'Agreed to be enforced by compulsory execution' column, please specify the targets that require compulsory execution, such as 'Return of the property at the end of the lease term', 'Payment of rent and penalty fees', 'Return of the security deposit', etc.
    • Finally, the request form should be signed and stamped by both parties, and the date should be recorded.
  3. After completing the request form, please register and submit it to the notary office for processing by a notary public.
  4. The requestor should bring their national ID card and seal, while the landlord should bring the property tax bill. If the requestor is a corporate legal entity, they should bring the company's business license, profit-seeking enterprise registration certificate (a copy is acceptable), the ID card of the person in charge, and the company and person in charge seals.
    • The authorization letter should be proven by one of the following methods:
      • Through notarization or certification.
      • Through certification by relevant government agencies, including village or neighborhood offices, the government agency where the requester is employed, the military unit where they are serving, or the prison where they are serving their sentence.
      • For documents created overseas, they must be authenticated by the ROC embassy or consulate abroad, or by an authorized organization or institution authorized by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. If the document is created in a foreign country, it must be authenticated by the embassy or consulate abroad and may be verified by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. If the document is created in mainland China, it must be authenticated by the Straits Exchange Foundation.
      • Documents prepared by foreign nationals or individuals residing abroad shall be certified.
      • The authorization letter should be accompanied by the seal certification of the requester (obtained from household registration office within the past six months), and the seal on the authorization letter should match the seal impression on the seal certification.
    • The seal certificate mentioned in item 5 above, for the "company" section, has stopped being issued by the competent authority since April 1, 1998, as the Ministry of Economic Affairs ceased issuing such certificates. In response to practical needs, according to the content of the letter with the reference number 87,7,10(87)YuanTaiMin-15392 from the Judicial Yuan, when a company appoints an agent to handle public or certified affairs, if the authorization letter is not accompanied by a seal certificate verified by a notary public or certified by the units specified in Article 22(2) of the old Notary Public Law, such as government offices, chambers of commerce, village and neighborhood offices, etc., the notary public shall not refuse to accept the request but should ask the requester to provide the following documents as a substitute for the seal certificate.
      • Please provide the original copy of the company registration certificate (for establishment or changes) issued by the competent authority within the last six months, along with a photocopy of the front and back of the certificate. The photocopy must be stamped with the company seal and the seal of its responsible person. The original certificate will be verified and returned without errors, and the photocopy will be kept for reference.
      • If more than six months have passed since the issuance of the original copy of the company registration certificate (for establishment or changes), in addition to providing the verified copy of the original card, a copy of the company registration certificate (for establishment or changes) issued by the competent authority within the last three months must be provided, along with supporting documents such as application for transcript or approval for changes. After verification without errors, the original copy will be returned, and the copy of the registration certificate and relevant supporting documents such as application for transcript or approval for changes will be kept for reference.
  5. For the required lease agreement, the requester is requested to bring at least three copies if they have them. If not, they can use the contracts printed by the notary public, which cost three dollars for every three copies.
  6. According to Article 80 of the Notary Public Act, if deemed necessary, the notary public shall physically inspect the property at its location.
  7. The notary fee shall be calculated by comparing the total rent or current value of the property tax, whichever is higher, plus the amount of the security deposit. This total amount shall be the subject matter value of the notary request. If the subject matter value is below 200,000 NT, the notary fee is 1,500 NT (because every lease agreement has compulsory enforcement effect); for subject matter values over 200,000 NT but below 500,000 NT, the notary fee is 3,000 NT; for subject matter values over 500,000 NT but below 1,000,000 NT, the notary fee is 4,500 NT; for subject matter values over 1,000,000 NT but below 2,000,000 NT, the notary fee is 6,000 NT. For more information, please refer to the website of the Judicial Yuan.

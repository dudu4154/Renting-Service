❏ Rental Subsidy Issues

The rental subsidy is the right of the tenant, and it is essential to thoroughly confirm whether the following issues exist.

➥ What should I do if the landlord refuses to allow the application for rental subsidy?

Landlords are not allowed to prohibit tenants from applying for rental subsidies. If a landlord insists on increasing rent or imposing additional taxes, tenants can file a complaint with the local government to request correction. Failure to rectify the situation may result in fines ranging from 30,000 to 300,000 NTD. For persistent non-compliance, the fines may be increased to 50,000 to 500,000 NTD, and penalties may be imposed repeatedly.

The Ministry of the Interior emphasizes that applying for rental subsidies is the right of tenants and does not require the landlord's approval. Landlords are also prohibited from preventing tenants from applying for rental subsidies. If a landlord unilaterally prohibits or requests abandonment of the application, the tenant can invoke the Consumer Protection Act Article 12 and Civil Code Article 247-1 to claim invalidity, as it is deemed unfair to the tenant.

For more details, please visit the Ministry of the Interior official website for more information.

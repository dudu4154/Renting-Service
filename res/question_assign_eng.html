❏ Contract Signing

Dear fellow students, please be reminded that it is important to read the lease carefully before signing. You should have the understanding that the law protects those who know how to defend their own rights. Therefore, trying to fight for your so-called 'reasonable rights' after the fact may be too late.

➥ Are different versions of contracts equally valid?

In general, there are various versions of contracts, such as those from bookstores, stationery stores, landlords, schools, and the Ministry of the Interior. Regardless of the version, as long as both parties agree and have no objections to the contents of the contract, and have signed and stamped it, the contract has legal effect and both parties are required to comply and fulfill their obligations.

➥ How to choose the appropriate contract?

Actually, as long as the terms of the contract do not violate public order, morals, or mandatory legal provisions, they can become a contract after both parties agree. Which version of the contract is better depends on personal needs. For example, for new houses, many additional equipment, or high rental prices, you may consider using a more detailed contract, such as the version provided by the Ministry of the Interior. On the other hand, for general residential use, it is better to choose a contract with fewer provisions. First, it is simple and easy to understand, and secondly, there is more room for negotiation, such as the version provided by Tsui Mama. Secondly, any part of the contract that is not agreed upon is processed according to the provisions of the "Civil Code" in legal procedures. Generally, the "Civil Code" takes into account both parties and considers fairness, which also protects tenants.

➥ My landlord is asking me to go to the court for 'notarization'... 'Notarization'? What is notarization and why do I have to do it?

"公證" refers to the judicial authority's certification of the existence of specific legal relationships or legal facts. The act of taking a lease agreement to the court for public notarization is to have the court act as a third-party witness to the agreement. The primary purpose of public notarization of a lease agreement is to ensure that when one party fails to perform the lease agreement, the other party can directly apply to the civil enforcement agency of the court for mandatory enforcement without having to go through a lengthy and costly legal process.

➥ Where can I handle the notarization procedure?

You can generally go to the local district court or a private notary public to handle this. For more information, please refer to the ' Contact Information for District Courts in Various Regions', ' Explanation of Notarial Affairs by the Judicial Yuan'.

➥ How to handle notarization of a lease agreement?

Please refer to 'How to Notarize a Rental Agreement' on the website of the Judicial Yuan.

➥ How are notarization fees calculated?

The notarization fee is calculated based on the higher value between the total rent amount and the current value of the property, plus the amount of the security deposit. This is the requested amount for the notarization. If the requested amount is below 200,000 NTD, the notarization fee is 1,500 NTD (as every lease agreement has compulsory enforcement effect). For other amounts, please refer to the Judicial Yuan website and the 'Notarization Fee Table'.

➥ Are contracts signed by minors legally binding? Is a third-party guarantor required?

According to Article 12 of the Civil Code, a person becomes an adult at the age of 20. Therefore, it is advisable for minors under the age of 20 to obtain the consent of their parents, legal guardians, or custodians when entering into contracts. Parents should act as joint guarantors when signing the contract to give landlords greater peace of mind when renting to underage students. However, if it can be established that the leased property is necessary for the minor's daily life, such as in the case of a student in pursuit of education, the lease agreement is still valid even without parental consent under the provision of Article 77 of the Civil Code.

➥ Is it necessary to sign a rental contract when renting a property?

In fact, most agreements can be established through oral agreements and do not necessarily require a written contract to be valid. However, it should be noted that according to Article 422 of the Civil Code: 'If the term of a lease contract for real property is over one year, it shall be made in writing. If it is not made in writing, it shall be deemed a lease without a fixed term.' However, since rental periods often involve related matters (such as early termination, repairs, etc.), if there is no written contract, there may be significant disputes between the parties regarding their rights and obligations, and it can be difficult to prove. Therefore, it is important to write down clearly the rights and obligations of both parties in a written contract to avoid disputes and prevent both parties from making unsupported claims, making it difficult for outsiders to clarify the truth of the matter.

➥ What are the implications for us if we only signed one contract with the landlord and the landlord kept the original copy?

It is best for both parties to have a copy of the signed contract to avoid situations where the tenant cannot review the terms of the contract or negotiate with the landlord based on the agreed-upon content when disputes arise. This can lead to a lack of security for the tenant.

Furthermore, both parties should sign any added or deleted clauses when signing the contract. If these procedures were not carried out in the original contract, tenants should be cautious about the landlord possibly making unauthorized changes to the contract, resulting in a loss of tenant rights.

Sometimes, landlords may also worry that tenants will use the contract to report rental income for tax purposes, so they will voluntarily "confiscate" one copy of the contract, making it difficult for tenants to claim deductions based on the contract. Or, the landlord may secretly raise the rent or terminate the lease, causing disputes in the future. However, this should be avoided to prevent conflicts.

➥ How can you confirm that the other party who signs the contract is indeed entitled to rent out the property?

When signing a contract, you can verify the landlord's identity by checking their ID card, property ownership certificate, or property tax statement. However, it is common for someone else, such as a spouse, daughter, or friend, to handle the rental process on behalf of the owner. This does not necessarily mean that these people cannot sign a contract with you, but if you find that the signer is not the actual owner, you should clarify their relationship and confirm that the owner is aware of the situation. You can also request that the landlord bring the actual owner to sign the contract or provide a power of attorney to avoid falling victim to rental fraud.

Of course, you can also use the rental address to apply for a land and building registration copy from the land administration agency to verify the landlord. This can also check whether the rental location is currently under seizure (including seizure time, the court accepting the case, and the case number, etc.) or has any mortgage records. This will allow you to rent with peace of mind.

➥ How can I confirm if the rented house is seized or not?

When verifying with the landlord, you can apply for a land and building record copy from the land administration agency using the address of the property (preferably with the landlord or owner's name). This not only allows you to check if there is a mortgage or if the property has been seized by a court (pay special attention to the time of seizure, as any rental agreement made after the seizure may be invalidated by the court), but also confirms if the owner of the property is the same person who signed the contract.

➥ During the signing of the contract, if the landlord asks to keep a copy of our ID, can I refuse? Is there any danger if I leave a copy?

The landlord may request to retain a photocopy of our ID card, which is probably because they are concerned about any situation that may occur during the lease period and the difficulty of finding tenants without any proof. Similarly, both landlords and tenants can make the same request. However, there may be some concerns about leaving personal identification documents in someone else's hands. If you are worried about identity theft, you can annotate the photocopy with the words "For Rental Use Only".

➥ What should you pay attention to when signing a contract with a sub-landlord?

When renting from a sub-landlord, it is important to ask for the lease agreement between the sub-landlord and the primary landlord, and to check whether the lease period has expired to avoid paying the deposit and rent only to find that the sub-landlord has moved out and cannot be found. It is also important to note whether there are any clauses in the agreement prohibiting subletting or assigning of the property, as the sub-landlord has no right to sublet the property to a third party if such a clause exists.

Since the rental rights and interests of the subtenant only involve the sublandlord, there may be disputes if the landlord asks the subtenant to move out or pay the deposit based on the agreement with the sublandlord. It is recommended to avoid such situations as much as possible. If you want to sublet one of the rooms in the rented property, it is recommended that the subtenant sign the lease directly with the landlord (of course, a new agreement or an adjustment in the original contract should be made between the landlord and the sublandlord regarding the rental scope and rent, for example) to avoid disputes.

➥ What should I do if the landlord refuses to sign a lease agreement?

Standardized lease agreements typically include provisions on rent, lease duration, deposit, maintenance responsibilities, and other aspects, helping to clarify the rights and obligations of both parties. The lack of a formal contract may jeopardize the tenant's rights, so it is advisable to sign a comprehensive lease agreement.

If the landlord is unwilling to sign the agreement, it may involve the following legal provisions:

  • Contract Law: A lease agreement is a type of contract, and the foundational law governing contracts is the Contract Law in the Civil Code. If the landlord fails to fulfill the contract or refuses to sign a formal lease agreement, it may violate relevant provisions of the Contract Law.
  • Lease Regulations: Various cities and counties in Taiwan usually have corresponding lease regulations, including details such as rent payment, lease duration, deposit requirements, and housing maintenance responsibilities. If the landlord violates local lease regulations, they may face corresponding legal consequences.
  • Land Value Tax Regulations: House leasing may also be related to land value tax. Violating the relevant land value tax regulations could lead to the landlord facing corresponding legal consequences.

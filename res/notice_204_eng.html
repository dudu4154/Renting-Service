Common Rental Disputes

Oops... it's not looking good, you and your landlord just can't seem to agree on anything, what can you do to solve this? Don't worry, below are some great tips that can help you avoid going to court and come out on top!

1. After signing the lease, can I request a reduction in rent on the grounds of non-use?

If it is due to uncontrollable external factors such as natural disasters or third-party intrusion, or due to the landlord's problems such as the inability to repair or tidy up the property, the tenant can make such a request. However, if it is due to personal reasons such as hospitalization, imprisonment, or going abroad, the request must be following the agreement specified in the lease. Therefore, students who do not stay during summer or winter breaks and wish not to pay rent must coordinate with the landlord. Otherwise, they should find ways to stay in the rental property to avoid feeling regretful.

2. Right to demand the return of the rental deposit.

The purpose of the security deposit is to guarantee the tenant's obligation to pay rent and compensate for damages in the lease agreement. Therefore, only after the lease term is over, the tenant has the right to request the return of the security deposit from the landlord.

3. Keep an eye on real estate agencies.

A good real estate agent can make finding a place to live easy and convenient, but it can be troublesome when dealing with dishonest agents. When looking for an agent, the first thing to do is to make sure that they have indeed been commissioned by the landlord. When signing the contract and paying the deposit, it is important to confirm the identity of the landlord or their authorized agent. It is best to have a professional agent handle this process, and if the process is fair and just, it is more secure. Also, make sure to pay the service fee only after all the rental procedures have been completed, so that you can do it willingly.

4. Is the tenant responsible for the housing tax?

According to the Civil Code, all taxes and fees related to the lease should be borne by the landlord. This means that the landlord is responsible for paying all property taxes, and the tenant is not required to pay them.

5. Is it illegal for me to sublet the house to someone else and become a 'sub-landlord'?

Tenants are not allowed to sublet the rental property to others without the landlord's consent, but they can sublet a portion of it to others. If the tenant wants to sublet the entire property, it must be specified in the original lease agreement. If the tenant violates the subletting agreement, the landlord can terminate the lease at any time, and the tenant cannot object. If the landlord does not terminate the lease, then there is no issue. However, if the landlord terminates the lease, the sub-tenant not only cannot become the primary tenant, but also may have to compensate for any losses incurred. It's a risky move!

6. When should a tenant report any damages to the rented property or furniture?

As soon as the tenant discovers any damage to the rental property or furniture, they should immediately notify the landlord. If the tenant fails to take timely remedial action and causes damage to the landlord, the tenant must compensate the landlord for their losses.

7. Can landlords withhold tenants' belongings?

In accordance with the law, landlords have the right to withhold tenants' belongings until all debts are paid. This is known as the landlord's 'right of retention.' However, not all items can be withheld by the landlord, such as basic necessities like clothing, bedding, and cooking utensils, or tools necessary for a tenant's profession. Other items can be withheld by the landlord as long as they are located within the rental property, and there is no need to apply to the court for this action, which does not constitute a restriction on freedom.

8. When signing a lease agreement, you cannot be careless!

A lease agreement requires careful consideration and scrutiny. In order to protect one's own rights and interests, one must pay attention to every word and detail, and not hastily sign their name on the contract upon receiving it. It is best to take it home and study it carefully, and not be limited by the contents of the contract. As long as the contract has not been signed, there is room for negotiation.

9. Can the lessee refuse to assign the lease if the security deposit has not been returned yet?

The Civil Code stipulates that tenants cannot refuse to return the leased property to the landlord simply because the security deposit has not been refunded. If there is a delay in returning the leased property, any resulting damages to the landlord during this period will be deducted from the security deposit.

10. Can a tenant deduct the cost of repairing the leased property from the rent?

If a tenant wants to repair and deduct the cost from the rent, they should notify the landlord to make the repair within a reasonable period of time. If the landlord fails to make the repair within the specified time, the tenant can then proceed with the repair and deduct the cost from the rent. However, the repair cost should not be excessive and should be in line with the market rate. The tenant should keep the receipts and inform the landlord as soon as possible, attaching a copy of the receipts.

11. Can landlords enter tenants' houses arbitrarily?

The landlord does not have the right to enter without prior notice and should respect the tenant's privacy.

12. Can landlords arbitrarily raise the rent?

Rent cannot be arbitrarily increased during fixed-term lease.

13. Can the landlord claim compensation if the tenant maliciously damages the property?

Article 432, Paragraph 1 of the Civil Code stipulates that 'The lessee shall take good care of the leased property as a prudent manager would.' Paragraph 2 further provides that 'If the lessee violates the obligation mentioned in the preceding paragraph, and causes damage or loss to the leased property, he/she shall be liable for damages.'

14. Can the landlord take back the house for personal use at any time?

The first half of Article 450, paragraph 2 of the Civil Code stipulates that 'where no fixed term is specified, either party may terminate the contract at any time.' In other words, fixed-term leases cannot be terminated at any time.

15. Is the landlord obliged to rent to the tenant after receiving a deposit?

If the tenant fails to sign the contract within the agreed-upon timeframe, the landlord may retain their deposit and lease the property to someone else.
